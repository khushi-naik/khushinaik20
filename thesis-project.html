Project Overview

I designed and developed an Augmented Reality (AR) display for vital signs, aimed at improving situational awareness in clinical settings like Intensive Care Units (ICU) and Operating Rooms (OR), where traditional bedside monitors rely heavily on auditory alarms and peripheral screens.

This system visualizes three critical vital signs: heart rate (HR), blood pressure (BP), and oxygen saturation (SpO₂) — using animated, color-coded 3D spheres that are always in the clinician’s field of view via a Head-Mounted Display (HoloLens 2).

Vital Sign Monitors

what are they anyway...?

Physiological parameters such as blood pressure (BP), heart rate (HR), and oxygen saturation (SpO₂) are critical indicators of a patient’s health condition.

Vital signs monitors continuously display these vitals and issue visual and/ or auditory alarms to alert staff about health deterioration.

BACKGROUND

Alarm Fatigue & Noise Overload

ICUs and ORs are filled with constant alarms and conversations, which can overwhelm clinicians and disturb resting patients.

Limited Visibility & Accessibility

Sign monitors are often not in the clinician’s direct line of sight, causing delays and distraction due to constant shifts in focus between patient and screen.

Challenges in Critical Field Settings

In military evacuations or space-constrained scenarios, bright screens and alarm sounds can compromise safety, while bulky equipment strains limited resources.

Study Results

To evaluate the effectiveness of our AR display in improving alarm saliency, we conducted a two-week-long within-subjects user study comparing our AR display to a mock conventional monitor interface in a controlled lab setting.

My Learnings

The value of user feedback – Deep insights into the intricacies of their daily tasks and helped me design with real-world needs in mind.

Designing for emerging technology – Hands-on experience creating user interactions for AR applications using Unity and HoloLens.

Experiment setup matters – Attention to detail in designing the study setup is just as crucial as the application itself, to ensure valid results.

Unexpected insights from user studies – Even with a carefully crafted design, revealed surprising user requirements.
