Get to Know Me

Engineer by training, Designer by passion, Researcher by choice

I’m a Master's student in Electrical Engineering at McGill University, Montreal, currently conducting research as part of the Shared Reality Lab, under the supervision of Professor Jeremy Cooperstock. My academic and professional journey sits at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (User Experience (UX) and Interaction Design, Augmented Reality (AR)) and Software Development. I am also very enthusiastic about AI ethics and governance.

My research focuses on designing and developing an AR-based alternative to conventional vital monitors, aimed at improving how critical patient alarms are communicated in high-stakes clinical settings. The project seeks to enhance situational awareness for healthcare professionals and contribute to better patient outcomes.

Before grad school, I worked as a Software Engineer at Ericsson R&D, India, gaining 1.5 years of hands-on industry experience in building scalable software systems. I’ve also held internships at ECL in Auckland and Jajal Medical, where I worked on UX design for 360 Virtual tours and AR visualization for healthcare, respectively.

In a nutshell, I’m here to build tech that is grounded in real human needs..

Passion Projects & Hobbies

I’m passionate about all things art and in my free time, I love to decompress with a canvas, some paints, and a set of brushes. Creativity has always been a big part of who I am. At IIIT Vadodara, I was a core member of the art club (Shades) and participated in several inter-university competitions (and won a few too!). I now enjoy weaving that same design sensibility into my academic and personal projects.

When I’m not painting, you’ll probably find me lost in a good fictional read. I also love to travel—getting to know new cultures, trying unfamiliar foods, and some photography too. Speaking of food, I like to cook as well. Do I strictly follow recipes? Let’s just say… it’s more of a creative process.

I also love learning new languages—it's part survival skill, part fun brag. I’m currently learning French and Korean, mostly so I can get through new places a little easier (and yes, order food with confidence).
